Bowdon v Chester 4

Oct 15th, 2014 | 2014, Nick West, league | Comments

After the previous weeks heroics against the student horde of North Wales could the 5th XI continue their march up the table.

The drama started the day before with messages filtering through Facesplat that Carl ‘strongside’ Bailey had been locked out of his flat & was in need of a home shirt. Would he be able to aquire one & get to the Grammar for the early meet-time?

Max remembered to bring his helmet & to the shock of everyone Manters turned up for an away game, we suspect he misread the fixture a being a home game at the Grammar. Having brushed off his protestations that Chester is not in his backyard we did point out that it wasn’t Wales & a passport wouldn’t be needed. The troops set off, whilst Sergeant Wilson Captain Ginger & Captain Mainwaring Manager Kyle waited for Mr Bailey & his missing shirt to arrive.

We arrived at Chester to be informed that Carl had still not found his shirt & was still hunting for it all over South Manchester. This forced the manager to put his kit on & become part of the playing squad again, thus leaving us with 11 fit players & 2 half crocked players on the bench (Ben F & Kyle).

The game started with some good interplay by the 2 teams, noted by Jake & Will linking up well. During one of Chester’s early attacks Jiggles decided to turn his ankle leaving the 5th’s with only 11 fit players. It was at this time that I’m reliably informed that Manters scored form ½ inch, I think it was either Will C or it could have been from one of Jake’s jinking runs who slipped the ball past the onrushing keeper but I was distracted by Jiggles hobbling past. During a couple of early attacks by Chester were thwarted by Max in goal & the tough tackling from the defence. Later in the first half Manters doubled his & Bowdon’s goal tally this time from ¼ inch.

The second half started with Bowdon pushing forward & Manters was surprised to receive the ball a foot out & so subsequently missed out on getting his hat-trick. Bowdon’s 3rd goal came from Ben F ghosting to the back post & scoring from 2 inches out. Late in the 2nd half Kyle decided that his presence was needed on the pitch, he muttered something about showing Manters how to score from the edge of the D.

Chester were thwarted at the other end by Max on multiple occasions but he was finally beaten by a well worked short corner. Final score 3-1.

Man of the match

Max (another great performance from a young keeper)
