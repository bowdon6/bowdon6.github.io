Bowdon v Knutsford 2

Oct 28th, 2014 | 2014, Dale Jago, league | Comments

A week after a result that everybody wanted to forget, the mighty Bowdon 5th team lined up against Knutsford on what was possibly the slowest pitch known to man.

With the young Callum Townley at left back and James Mazarelo bringing the average age of the team down significantly, the team was eager to start and get back to winning ways.

The team started well and were dominant for around the first 10 minutes, pressing Knutsford early on down the right hand side through Breton and then on to Will “covers the whole pitch in his sleep” Corless on a number of occasions. Equally on the left and in the centre, Jago and Braithwaite were given some decent passes from Townley and Abbott to make Knutsford work hard to gain possession.

During the next 5 minutes or so, Knutsford did press hard and made West and Abbott work hard defensively. There were a couple of soft goal attempts but nothing Mazarelo couldn’t handle.

Then came the Doctors time to shine! Having been on the pitch for a few minutes and running around like a headless chicken, the valium that Mosedale had prescribed himself must have finally kicked in and he settled down to glide unnoticed past a defender like a ninja and get on the end of a Manterfield pass to slot in from a yard away (although the defender did try pretty hard to stop it).

Soon after, Braithwaite found Jago at the top of the D with a cracking pass who then managed to drag a defender out to the right and pass to Nick “Deadly from 5 yards” Manterfield to slot home Bowdon’s second.

After the restart Manterfield again found the back of the net after waiting for the keeper to come out and then casually flicking it through.

I am reliably informed that Braithwaite then got a goal but I honestly can’t remember this!

Knutsford did get a couple of goals back and looked Dangerous in the last 10 minutes of the game. Mosedale had an open goal and managed to hit the post which would have earned him Dick of the Day had Jago not got his feet tangled up and miss an even easier open goal attempt.

Special mention goes to all of the defence who had to work hard throughout the game to make sure we only conceded 2. Fallon Junior was as usual terrorizing the opposition and setting up some good moves.

Man of the Match

Will Corless who once again seemed to be everywhere. We need to get a heat map for him for the next game as I don’t think there is a part of the pitch he doesn’t hit!

Dick of the Day Award

Jago for the absolute hash of a shot on goal (if you can even call it that). It was simply terrible.

Next week sees us back at Chester to take on their 5th team which will be a tough game. Let’s keep up the good work and get a solid result.
