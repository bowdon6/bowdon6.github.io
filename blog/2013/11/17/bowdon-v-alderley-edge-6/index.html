Bowdon v Alderley Edge 6

Nov 17th, 2013 | 2013, Neil Breton, league | Comments

From our man in Altrincham, Neil Breton

At Captain Kyle’s request, the squad turned up for the match 45 before push back. This meant that 15 minutes before push back we’d run out of drills to do, so a lengthy team talk resulted to fill a bit of time.

The first half saw the results of the practice plain for all to see: probably the worst half of hockey played all season. Poor marking, passing and only using half the pitch saw Alderley dominating possession with pass options throughout the midfield and overlaps down both sides.

Can’t remember the first goal, only that the guy who scored had about 10 yards of space and time to pick his spot which he duly did to deservedly give Alderley the edge (apologies). This prompted us to remember how hockey should be played, and a flowing move lead to Carl calmly rounding the keeping and finishing well on the reverse side past a guy on the line. We then forgot how to play again, allowing the Alderley player to run to the top of the D like we do at training and strike the ball 2 inches into the D (like we don’t do at training).

A stirring half time talk that I can’t remember sparked a typical second half revival. Much better from the off, passing was crisper, movement better and we remembered to mark when Alderley had the ball.

Senior Brooks remembered the right half of the pitch existed and a raking, accurate, cross field ball found your truly in acres of space. A dart into the box was followed by a nerveless (flukey) finish off the inside of the far past. Some more good play followed, with OG making another popular appearance on the scoresheet following a goalmouth scramble. Some perceived inconsistent decision making by the away umpire saw your reporter green carded for dissent (sorry team). A long range shot from someone (i was too far away to see who) flicked either an outfield player or their keeper on its way into the back of the goal (see above), meaning OG struck again.

4-2 was a fair result for our second half performance, and if we play as we did in the second half then we’ve a good chance next week.
