Bowdon v Timperley

Oct 28th, 2013 | 2013, Nick West, league | Comments

From the imagination of Mr N West…

The time had arrived once again for the 6th XI to step out onto the field of play.

How had the young pretender managed to galvanise the Ginger Maestro’s squad from a mid table team into title contenders? Some might say it’s the introduction of his younger brother, or has his minion Kyle installed alarms in the house which allow the skipper to arrive before the rest of the squad?

With the news that half the usual back 4 (Disco Pete & Chris G), it turned out that Dale ‘The Dribbler’ Jago & Rick ‘The Stinger’ Prosser had been drafted in as defensive cover. Could The Dribbler curb his instincts & maintain the discipline needed to keep our unbeaten record intact.

With young Ben & Andy B controlling the midfield Kyle & Neil were able to advance on either flank. Although their crosses were often met by our forward duo they were ably kept out by the athleticism of the young Timperley keeper.

Unfortunately Timperley did manage to bypass the Ginger Maestro & Ben which meant yours truly was forced to deflect the ball skilfully (or was it luck) past our goal. It also led to me being given a green card, apparently putting your foot into the path of the ball is not the done thing!!! (I think it was probably the complete lack of subtly that did for you. Your ‘Who me?’ look was good though - AB)

After some good interplay between Ben & Dave B the ball was put into the D for Andy Gadsby to bag a goal. Half time score 1 0.

The 2nd half began with some neat interplay between Dave, Neil, Ben & Andy G. These attacks were either snuffed out by the Timperley defence or put wide of the post. Kyle decided try & garner another nomination for DOTD by getting ahead of some of Neil’s crosses. Unfortunately for he lost out to Neil who let out a girly scream when the Timperley keeper launched the ball in the direction of his dodgy knee. (In Neil’s defence, the ball must have gone within 20m of him - AB)

Timperley managed to get forward on several occasions when Dale decided that playing at right back meant he could dribble from our own 22. This forced both Frank & myself to get stuck in & again I was forced to use skill to deflect the ball just out of reach of the young whipper snapper at the far post. It also meant that Rick had to stop the opposition right wing by stepping into the path of his run.

Once Kyle’s Ibuprofen / Paracetamol half time energy drink kicked in 30 minutesinto the 2nd half he created an opening which Andy G neatly converted to make the score 2 0.

Next week we’re back on the hallowed turf against Knutsford…..
