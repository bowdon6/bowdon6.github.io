Bowdon 6 v Bangor 2s

Oct 6th, 2012 | 2012, Neil Breton, league | Comments

Brought to you this week by Neil ‘Goal Machine’ Breton:

The second longest pier in Wales.

The first away match of the season saw the 6th’s embarking on a long and perilous journey to Middle Earth (if last weeks match report is to be believed). Home of Wales’ second longest pier (thanks Andy), Bangor was this week’s destination.

The first test of the day was the pre-dawn meet time. Unexpectedly, Fallon was the last man did arrive.

Despite the closure of the A55 and Andy’s insistence on using Apple maps, all 11 men made it the pitch on time and promptly set about avoiding any structured warm up or drills, preferring to watch Bangor go through their paces.

Frank – this is what 09:45 looks like. The big hand is on 9, the little hand is between 9 and 10…

A rousing pre-match talk from El Capitano – “if we let them run around they might tire themselves out and we could nick a point” gave the team hope. The first half passed without incident, Bowdon’s well organised defence keeping Bangor at bay, forcing them to ping the ball sideways at the half way line without really making any progress into the Bowdon 16. Highlights included Andy intimidating a Bangor midfielder so much he threw himself to the floor in terror, and a great save from Dave from a short corner.

An inspirational half time speech – “we’ve not got a point yet. Yes we’ll get tired, but we can hold on” set Bowdon up for the second half. An increasingly frustrated Bangor team kept trying to pick killer passes, creating more chances as Bowden tired. Having used one of their three available substitute goalkeepers and with 2 rolling substitutes, Bangor kept pushing and enjoyed a majority of pressure and territory. This counted for nothing however when the ball got shelled up the left wing (possibly from a 16, I can’t really remember) to Neil who turned his defender like he was a line of cones at training and struck a weak shot from the top of the D that hobbled over the keepers stick at the near post. The crowd went silent, Bangor went silent, and Bowdon couldn’t believe that Breton had scored his bi-annual goal at such a key moment. 5 minutes later the ball was in the back of the net again, Andy’s reverse stick effort from 3 yards disallowed as Dave Brooks was too lazy to get out the way.

This fired up the Bangor team, who set up camp in the Bowdon half. Long balls out were held up well by Dave who had no support. Until he got yellow carded for a cynical goal-saving tackle. One last short corner at the final whistle gave Bangor a final role of the dice, but the shot was well saved, and Bowdon held on to steal 3 points from a gutted Bangor team.

Special mention must go to the two umpires who did a great job and were class.

Over a decent sausage and chip lunch at the local student bar, Frank Fallon was voted man of the match despite turning up late. – I vetoed this, he can be MOM when he pays his fine…
